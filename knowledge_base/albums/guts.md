# GUTS

- **Album Name:** GUTS
- **Artist:** Keshi
- **Release Date:** September 13, 2024
- **Genre:** Alternative R&B, indie pop
- **Tracklist (5-10 tracks):**
  - Say
  - Soft Spot
  - Night
  - Overglow
  - Dreamstate
  - Bodies
- **Production Notes:** The album expands Keshi’s sonic palette with fuller live instrumentation, cleaner low-end definition, and cinematic layering around his intimate vocal style. Compared with his earlier work, the arrangements feel broader and more performance-ready, but they still leave enough negative space for his whispered phrasing and melodic melancholy to carry the emotional weight. Lyrically, *GUTS* leans into vulnerability, attachment, self-doubt, and romantic afterimages, framing emotional exposure as something uncomfortable but necessary rather than merely aesthetic.
- **Chart Performance:** Generated strong streaming activity and helped sustain Keshi’s position as a globally viable album artist rather than only a singles-driven act.
- **Critical Reception:** Critics responded positively to the record’s maturity, particularly its balance between atmospheric writing and more direct melodic structure. More specific reactions often emphasized how the album widened Keshi's sonic scope without dulling his intimacy, with tracks like "Soft Spot" and "Dreamstate" commonly treated as examples of his ability to scale up production while keeping the writing personal.
- **Awards:** Appeared on several year-end alternative pop and R&B recommendation lists and strengthened Keshi’s profile in international festival and touring conversations.

## Standout Tracks

- **"Soft Spot"** - A tender, melody-first song that distills the album's mix of vulnerability and polish into one of its most immediate moments.
- **"Night"** - A moodier cut whose atmosphere and pacing show Keshi leaning deeper into cinematic late-night production.
- **"Dreamstate"** - A lush, emotionally suspended track that highlights the album's broader arrangements without losing its inward focus.
- **"Bodies"** - A more textured song that gives the record some physical momentum while keeping its romantic unease intact.

*GUTS* feels like a deliberate expansion rather than a reinvention. It preserves Keshi’s quiet emotional core while opening the arrangements enough to support bigger stages, making it a natural progression from the tone established on *Skeletons*.

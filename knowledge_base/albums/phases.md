# Phases

- **Album Name:** Phases
- **Artist:** Chase Atlantic
- **Release Date:** June 28, 2019
- **Genre:** Alternative pop, dark R&B
- **Tracklist (5-10 tracks):**
  - Into It
  - Her
  - Love Is (Not) Easy
  - Paranoid
  - Stuckinmybrain
  - Heaven and Back
  - No Rainbows
- **Production Notes:** The album emphasizes nocturnal synth design, bass-heavy programming, and emotionally unstable storytelling, but it is more detailed than simple mood music. Chase Atlantic blends pop structure with darker textures by stacking trap-influenced drums, processed vocals, guitar lines, and occasional saxophone coloring into songs that feel glossy and half-unraveled at the same time. Lyrically, *Phases* circles obsession, intoxication, jealousy, lust, alienation, and self-sabotage, presenting romance as something thrilling and corrosive in equal measure.
- **Chart Performance:** Built steady momentum through streaming and fan circulation, becoming one of the group’s most recognized full-length releases and a reliable entry point for new listeners.
- **Critical Reception:** Fans and critics alike viewed the album as a strong distillation of the band’s identity, praising its mood consistency and crossover appeal. More specific appraisals tended to highlight how songs like "Heaven and Back" translated the band's dark-pop aesthetic into something highly replayable, while cuts such as "Paranoid" and "Stuckinmybrain" were often cited as proof that the trio could maintain atmosphere without losing pop structure.
- **Awards:** While not a major awards-driven release, it significantly elevated the band’s profile and is often cited as a breakthrough album in retrospective discussions.

## Standout Tracks

- **"Heaven and Back"** - A fan-favorite single that pairs damaged romantic writing with one of the band's strongest pop hooks.
- **"Paranoid"** - A tense, spiraling track whose production captures the album's fixation on anxiety and emotional instability.
- **"Stuckinmybrain"** - A sleek late-night cut that leans hard into Chase Atlantic's intoxicating, self-destructive lyrical persona.
- **"No Rainbows"** - A brooding closer that leaves the record on a darker, more disillusioned note than its most immediate songs suggest.

*Phases* captures Chase Atlantic at a moment when their sound became fully recognizable: moody, stylish, and emotionally reckless. Its cohesion helped turn a growing online following into long-term fan loyalty.

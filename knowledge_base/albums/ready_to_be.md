# Ready To Be

- **Album Name:** Ready To Be
- **Artist:** TWICE
- **Release Date:** March 10, 2023
- **Genre:** K-pop, dance-pop, pop
- **Tracklist (5-10 tracks):**
  - Set Me Free
  - Moonlight Sunrise
  - Got the Thrills
  - Blame It on Me
  - Wallflower
  - Crazy Stupid Love
- **Production Notes:** The album leans into confident, stage-ready pop with larger hooks, sharper rhythmic emphasis, and a more mature vocal presentation. Its production is cleaner and more muscular than TWICE's early work, using disco-pop shimmer, assertive drum programming, and layered choruses designed for arena lift rather than only cute immediacy. Lyrically, the record centers on agency, attraction, self-possession, and emotional commitment, framing TWICE as a group speaking from confidence rather than first-crush uncertainty.
- **Chart Performance:** Opened strongly in multiple territories, including notable U.S. chart visibility, and supported a major world tour cycle that expanded the group’s global profile.
- **Critical Reception:** Reviews described the record as polished and self-assured, praising TWICE for refining their mature-era sound without abandoning the melodic immediacy fans expect. More detailed write-ups often highlighted "Set Me Free" as a convincing large-stage opener, while b-sides like "Wallflower" and "Blame It on Me" were cited as evidence that the group could stretch into darker or more dramatic textures without sounding forced.
- **Awards:** Earned year-end shortlist placements and strong recognition within fan-voted categories tied to touring and pop performance.

## Standout Tracks

- **"Set Me Free"** - A confident title track that pairs disco-pop momentum with one of the group's clearest statements of romantic agency.
- **"Moonlight Sunrise"** - A sleek English-language single that leans warmer and more sensual, giving the release a strong crossover pop entry point.
- **"Wallflower"** - A tightly produced b-side whose restrained groove and understated mood show TWICE's mature style working without a giant chorus.
- **"Blame It on Me"** - A more dramatic, almost rock-tinged cut that adds tension and edge to an otherwise glossy record.

*Ready To Be* functions as a clear statement of TWICE’s stadium-era identity. Rather than reinventing the group, it sharpens what they already do well: high-energy singles, clean vocal contrasts, and an emotional brightness that still comes through even in their more dramatic tracks.

# The Name Chapter: TEMPTATION

- **Album Name:** The Name Chapter: TEMPTATION
- **Artist:** TXT / Tomorrow X Together
- **Release Date:** January 27, 2023
- **Genre:** K-pop, pop-rock, alternative pop
- **Tracklist (5-10 tracks):**
  - Devil by the Window
  - Sugar Rush Ride
  - Happy Fools
  - Tinnitus (Wanna be a rock)
  - Farewell, Neverland
- **Production Notes:** The album combines dreamlike synth textures, rhythmic experimentation, and emotionally theatrical songwriting in a way that makes temptation feel seductive before it feels dangerous. Across the tracklist, TXT and their collaborators move between whispered tension, tropical and Afropop accents, trap-inflected percussion, and sweeping melodic release, giving the project a restless but carefully sequenced shape. Lyrically, the record focuses on escape, desire, fantasy, and the consequences of wanting refuge from reality, treating temptation less as a simple thrill than as a fragile emotional bargain.
- **Chart Performance:** Debuted with strong global sales and streaming numbers, reinforcing TXT’s standing as one of the most internationally competitive boy groups of their generation.
- **Critical Reception:** Widely praised for thematic clarity, sequencing, and the group’s ability to blend concept-heavy storytelling with accessible hooks. "Tinnitus" and "Sugar Rush Ride" were especially noted for stylistic risk, while broader reviews often pointed to the album's concise runtime as a strength because it let every sonic pivot feel intentional rather than overexplained.
- **Awards:** Collected numerous year-end nominations and was frequently cited in best-of-the-year K-pop album discussions.

## Standout Tracks

- **"Sugar Rush Ride"** - A slippery title track that moves from seductive restraint into a more explosive chorus, capturing the album's central push-pull between pleasure and danger.
- **"Tinnitus (Wanna be a rock)"** - A rhythmically distinctive b-side whose dancehall and Afropop influences made it one of the release's most discussed stylistic departures.
- **"Devil by the Window"** - A tense opener that establishes the album's darker fairytale mood through layered vocals and creeping production.
- **"Farewell, Neverland"** - A closing song that trades fantasy for emotional reckoning, giving the project a reflective landing after its more intoxicating middle stretch.

*The Name Chapter: TEMPTATION* is one of TXT’s most cohesive records, presenting temptation as both fantasy and collapse. The project deepened the group’s artistic identity while remaining one of their most immediately replayable releases.
